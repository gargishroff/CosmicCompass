actual wavelength = the wavelength that the object should be displaying. apparent wavelength = the wavelength that the object appears to have. c = the speed of light. the doppler effect is a change in frequency due to radial motion. it lets us calculate an object’s radial velocity. as a spacecraft is moving, the light from the stars in front of the ship appears to be shifted toward the blue end of the spectrum (blueshift). this is because as each crest arrives at the spacecraft the spacecraft has moved a little closer towards the star. the crests arrive faster than they should. they are closer together, the frequency is higher, and appears to have moved in the blue direction. light from the stars behind the ship appears to be shifted toward the red end of the spectrum (redshift). this is because as each crest arrives at the spacecraft the spacecraft has moved a little further away from the star. the crests arrive slower than they should. they are further apart, the frequency is lower, and appears to have moved in the red direction. this is the same effect we hear with sound waves. police sirens, train whistles, and other noises change from a higher frequency as the object is approaching to a lower frequency after the object has passed.
