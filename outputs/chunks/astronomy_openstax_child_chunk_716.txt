(credit: apollo 11 crew, nasa) unlike the mountains on earth, the moon’s highlands do not have any sharp folds in their ranges. the highlands have low, rounded profiles that resemble the oldest, most eroded mountains on earth (figure 9.8). because there is no atmosphere or water on the moon, there has been no wind, water, or ice to carve them into cliffs and sharp peaks, the way we have seen them shaped on earth. their smooth features are attributed to gradual erosion, mostly due to impact cratering.
