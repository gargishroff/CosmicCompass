cygnus x-1 is in a binary star system, and the artist’s illustration on the right shows the black hole pulling material away from a massive blue companion star. this material forms a disk (shown in red and orange) that rotates around the black hole before falling into it or being redirected away from the black hole in the form of powerful jets. the material in the disk (before it falls into the black hole) is so hot that it glows with x-rays, explaining why this object is an x-ray source.
