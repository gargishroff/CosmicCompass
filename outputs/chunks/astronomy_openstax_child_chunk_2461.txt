(x-ray)) many highly accelerated particles move with the jets in such galaxies. along the way, the particles in the jets can plow into gas clouds in the interstellar medium, breaking them apart and scattering them. since denser clouds of gas and dust are required for material to clump together to make stars, the disruption of the clouds can halt star formation in the host galaxy or cut it off before it even begins. in this way, quasars and other kinds of agn can play a crucial role in the evolution of their galaxies. for example, there is growing evidence that the merger of two gas-rich galaxies not only produces a huge burst of star formation, but also triggers agn activity in the core of the new galaxy. that activity, in turn, could then slow down or shut off the burst of star formation—which could have significant implications for the apparent shape, brightness, chemical content, and stellar components of the entire galaxy.
