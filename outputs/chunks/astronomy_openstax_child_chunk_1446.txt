(credit: modification of work by mpia/v. joergens) most brown dwarfs start out with atmospheric temperatures and spectra like those of true stars with spectral classes of m6.5 and later, even though the brown dwarfs are not hot and dense enough in their interiors to fuse hydrogen. in fact, the spectra of brown dwarfs and true stars are so similar from spectral types late m through l that it is not possible to distinguish the two types of objects based on spectra alone. an independent measure of mass is required to determine whether a specific object is a brown dwarf or a very low mass star.
