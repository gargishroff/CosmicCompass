figure 23.19 compton detects gamma-ray bursts. (a) in 1991, the compton gamma-ray observatory was deployed by the space shuttle atlantis. weighing more than 16 tons, it was one of the largest scientific payloads ever launched into space. (b) this map of gamma-ray burst positions measured by the compton gamma-ray observatory shows the isotropic (same in all directions), uniform distribution of bursts on the sky. the map is oriented so that the disk of the milky way would stretch across the center line (or equator) of the oval. note that the bursts show no preference at all for the plane of the milky way, as many other types of objects in the sky do. colors indicate the total energy in the burst: red dots indicate long-duration, bright bursts; blue and purple dots show short, weaker bursts.
