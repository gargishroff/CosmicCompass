in every two revolutions around the sun there will be three rotations by mercury on its axis. this is caused by tidal effects from the gravity of the sun as well as gravitational tugs by the planets. perihelion precession each orbit of mercury around the sun results in a small precession of mercury’s perihelion. the precession is 1.5436° per century. newton’s laws can’t explain all of this precession. it was einstein’s theory of relativity that finally solved the missing piece.
