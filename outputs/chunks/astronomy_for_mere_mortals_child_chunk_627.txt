giovanni domenico cassini (1625-1712), an italian astronomer and astrologer, was hired by louis xiv of france in 1669 to establish an observatory [image: astronomy_for_mere_mortals_page447_img1.jpeg] astronomy for mere mortals – an introductory astronomy text 448 figure 490: distance to io in paris. as head of the new paris observatory, cassini hired an assistant, jean richer, and dispatched him to cayenne, just off the south american coast near french guyana. with richer in cayenne and cassini in paris making simultaneous angular measurements of the position of the planet mars, cassini calculated the earth-mars distance by parallax, or triangulation. then using kepler’s laws, he calculated the distance from the earth to the sun, or 1 au, the astronomical unit. cassini’s value of 140,000,000 km (about 87,000,000 miles), turns out to be off by less than 7%.
