(credit: modification of work by nasa) radiation intermediate between x-rays and visible light is ultraviolet (meaning higher energy than violet). outside the world of science, ultraviolet light is sometimes called “black light” because our eyes cannot see it. ultraviolet radiation is mostly blocked by the ozone layer of earth’s atmosphere, but a small fraction of ultraviolet rays from our sun do penetrate to cause sunburn or, in extreme cases of overexposure, skin cancer in human beings. ultraviolet astronomy is also best done from space. electromagnetic radiation with wavelengths between roughly 400 and 700 nm is called visible light because these are the waves that human vision can perceive. this is also the band of the electromagnetic spectrum that most readily reaches earth’s surface. these two observations are not coincidental: human eyes evolved to see the kinds of waves that arrive from the sun most effectively.
