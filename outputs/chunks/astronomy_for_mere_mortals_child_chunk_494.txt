an example would be, rather than writing 300,000 km/sec for the speed of light, you could write it as 3 * 105 km/sec. the 5 in this number is called an exponent. getting an exponent. in our example, 300,000 is really “300,000.”. whether you see it or not, the decimal place is really there. the exponent is the number of places you move the decimal place to the right or to the left. • if you move the decimal place to the left, then the exponent will be a positive number. • if you move the decimal place to the right, then it will be a negative number. for 300,000, you moved it 5 places to the left. so you write 105. this really means 10 * 10 * 10 * 10 * 10 which is 100,000 so 3 * 105 is really 3 * 100,000, or the original 300,000. you normally move the decimal to either a convenient number to get the units you want, or you move it so that there is only one number to the left of the decimal place. exponents – you try it. 1.
