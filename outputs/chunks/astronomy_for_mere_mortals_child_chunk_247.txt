[image: astronomy_for_mere_mortals_page191_img1.jpeg] astronomy for mere mortals – an introductory astronomy text 192 figure 205: saturn's atmospheric profile figure 204: storms on saturn figure 203: saturn's atmosphere component 73 – saturn’s atmosphere composition saturn’s atmosphere is mostly composed of molecular hydrogen (96%) and helium (3%). atmospheric structure saturn’s lower atmosphere, the troposphere, consists of layers of ice. water ice is the bottom layer from -300 km to approximately -225 km. then there is a layer of ammonium hydrosulfide ice up to approximately - 160 km. and the top layer of ice is ammonia ice up to approximately -50 km. at 0 km there is a thick cloud layer. this haze layer is visible from earth. storms can be seen on saturn’s surface, but the thick upper layer of clouds makes markings much less obvious than on jupiter (see figure 204). saturn’s stratosphere continues upwards from the haze layer.
