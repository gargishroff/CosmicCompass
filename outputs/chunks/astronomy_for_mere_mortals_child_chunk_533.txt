galactic epoch from 200 million years to 3 billion years after the big bang. this is the time when large scale structure of the universe formed (galaxies and larger). galactic halo the sphere around a galaxy. astronomy for mere mortals – an introductory astronomy text 402 galaxy a very large collection of stars, all gravitationally bound together and orbiting a central black hole (usually). there are from millions to trillions of stars. galaxy cluster a group of galaxies that are gravitationally bound together. galaxy evolution the process by which a galaxy evolves through mergers from a small irregular galaxy, to one with a central black hole, then to a quasar, then to a radio galaxy or a seyfert galaxy, and finally into the elliptical and spiral galaxies we see today. galilean moons jupiter’s four large moons that are visible from earth in a pair of binoculars: io, europa, ganymede, and callisto. gamma ray bursts one of the most violent events in the universe. astronomers are not sure whether they are due to the merger of two neutron stars in a binary star system or if it is due to a stalled supernova restarting.
