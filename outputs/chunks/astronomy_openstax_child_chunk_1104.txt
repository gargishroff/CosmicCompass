as we will see later in this chapter, most jupiter-family comets come from a different source than the long- period comets (those with orbital periods longer than about a century). observational records exist for thousands of comets. we were visited by two bright comets in recent decades. first, in march 1996, came comet hyakutake, with a very long tail. a year later, comet hale-bopp appeared; it was as bright as the brightest stars and remained visible for several weeks, even in urban areas (see the image that opens this chapter, figure 13.1) since then, there have been few comets visible to the naked eye, and astronomers (both professional and amateur) eagerly await such a visitor. table 13.2 lists some well-known comets whose history or appearance is of special interest.
