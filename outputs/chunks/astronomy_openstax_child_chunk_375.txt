we can use these ideas to come up with a rough sort of “thermometer” for measuring the temperatures of stars. because many stars give off most of their energy in visible light, the color of light that dominates a star’s appearance is a rough indicator of its temperature. if one star looks red and another looks blue, which one has the higher temperature? because blue is the shorter-wavelength color, it is the sign of a hotter star.
