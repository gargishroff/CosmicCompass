to determine the distance, the change in angle of a star’s position (which has been measured using a baseline of ∼2 au) is converted into what is termed the parallax of the star which is the angu- lar shift in position of the star that would be observed with a baseline of exactly 1 au. as the angles are (very) small, one can then immediately derive its distance from θ ! a/d, where θ is the parallax in radians, a is the astronomical unit and d is the distance. if a is in kilometres, then d would also be in kilometres. figure 6.1 the method of parallax.
