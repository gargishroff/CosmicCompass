let’s stop for a moment and take in this figure: a single black hole that has swallowed enough material to make 3.5 billion stars like the sun. few astronomical measurements have ever led to so mind-boggling a result. what a strange environment the neighborhood of such a supermassive black hole must be. an international team of astronomers in 2019 made the first image of what the inner region of such a black hole, near the event horizon, looks like. they used the event horizon telescope, an array of radio telescopes in different countries, spanning planet earth, to observe m87. the signals from the many telescopes were sent to a central location and used to make a far more detailed radio-wave image than any single telescope can produce. figure 27.8b shows you that picture with artificial colors to help the eye. what you see as a black region in the middle is the shadow that the central black hole of m87 makes on its event horizon. another example is shown in figure 27.9. here, we see a disk of dust and gas that surrounds a 300-million-msun black hole in the center of an elliptical galaxy.
