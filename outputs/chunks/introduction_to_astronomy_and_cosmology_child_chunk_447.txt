6.12 the stellar mass–luminosity relationship stars on the main sequence have been found to have a well deﬁ ned mass–luminosity relationship. the luminosity rises steeply with mass. this is the result of the fact that, in order to support the mass of the overlying star, the pressures (and hence tempera- tures) of the star’s core have to increase as the star’s mass increases (figure 6.13). figure 6.12 the relative sizes of some typical stars.
