[image: introduction_to_astronomy_and_cosmology_page192_img1.jpeg] 172 introduction to astronomy and cosmology 5.8 the cassegrain telescope the majority of professional telescopes are a variant of a type of telescope called a cassegrain. in these telescopes the secondary mirror is a hyperboloid which reﬂ ects the light down through a central hole through the primary mirror to the focal plane. this is a far better place to locate heavy equipment such as a spectrometer. it has been pointed out that the image quality of a parabolic primary falls off rather rapidly away from the optical axis due to the optical aber- ration called coma. if, instead, the primary is also a hyperboloid in what is called a ritchey–chrétien telescope, coma is eliminated making it well suited for wide ﬁ eld observations. (this telescope was invented by george willis ritchey and henri chrétien in the early 1910s.) the vast majority of all professional telescopes, including the hubble space telescope, are of this design.
