table 12 shows transits of mercury through 2040. the times are in universal time. mercury’s greatest elongation as mercury orbits the sun it never strays far from the sun in the sky. this is because it is an inner planet, closer to the sun than the earth is. from our perspective mercury oscillates back and forth past the sun. at times it is a morning object and at other times it is an evening object. the points where it is furthest from the sun are referred to as greatest elongation. for mercury, the greatest elongation is 28 °.
