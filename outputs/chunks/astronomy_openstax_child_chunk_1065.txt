the majority of asteroids are in the asteroid belt, the region between mars and jupiter that contains all asteroids with orbital periods between 3.3 to 6 years (figure 13.2). although more than 75% of the known asteroids are in the belt, they are not closely spaced (as they are sometimes depicted in science fiction movies). the volume of the belt is actually very large, and the typical spacing between objects (down to 1 kilometer in size) is several million kilometers.
