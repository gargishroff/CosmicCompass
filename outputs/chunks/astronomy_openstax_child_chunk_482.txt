(credit: modification of work by eso, f.marchis, m.wong (uc berkeley); e.marchetti, p.amico, s.tordo (eso)) how astronomers really use telescopes in the popular view (and some bad movies), an astronomer spends most nights in a cold observatory peering through a telescope, but this is not very accurate today. most astronomers do not live at observatories, but near the universities or laboratories where they work. an astronomer might spend only a week or so each year observing at the telescope and the rest of the time measuring or analyzing the data acquired from large project collaborations and dedicated surveys. many astronomers use radio telescopes for space experiments, which work just as well during the daylight hours. still others work at purely theoretical problems using supercomputers and never observe at a telescope of any kind.
