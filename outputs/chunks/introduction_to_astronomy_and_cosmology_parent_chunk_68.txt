he found that the comets that had been seen in 1531, 1607 and 1682 had very similar orbital elements and so believed that these were three ‘apparitions’ of the same comet. halley could even account for the slight differ- ences by taking into account the gravitational effects of jupiter and saturn. its period was ∼76 years so he predicted that it would return around the end of 1757. more accurate calculations by three french mathematicians indicated that it would, in fact, pass closest to the sun in march 1759. it was ﬁ rst spotted by johann georg palitzsch, a german farmer and amateur astronomer on christmas day 1758. halley, who had died in 1742, did not live to see the comet’s return but it was named after him with the designation 1p/halley – the ‘1p’ indicating that it was the ﬁ rst known periodic comet.
