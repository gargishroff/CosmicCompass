it is believed that copernicus underwent minor orders, but did not become an ordained priest. after the death of his uncle in 1512, he was made a canon (an administrator) at frombork cathedral, a position he occupied for the rest of his life. it was at this time that he first went public, in a small way, with an outline called the commentariolus, where he introduced his heliocentric astronomy. the commentariolus was only circulated among a handful of friends, but by 1533 it had finally reached rome, where he was encouraged to enlarge and elaborate his system because of a growing interest in calendar reform by the church. but copernicus hesitated, not for fear of accusations of heresy (since the church was actually encouraging him), but for fear of ridicule. others before him had offered up new theories, only to be attacked and rejected by scholars and theologians. this might have been the end of it had not a young protestant professor at the university of wittenberg, georg rheticus, gotten wind of the theory and personally goaded copernicus into releasing the full version.
