a nasa/esa satellite mission with a rich website to explore. solar dynamics observatory: https://sdo.gsfc.nasa.gov/ (https://sdo.gsfc.nasa.gov/). about the mission to study the sun in many wavelengths. space weather live: https://www.spaceweatherlive.com/ (https://www.spaceweatherlive.com/). this allows you to keep almost instant track of what is happening with the sun and how its activity is likely to affect auroras here on earth. stanford solar center: https://solar-center.stanford.edu/ (https://solar-center.stanford.edu/). an excellent site with information for students and teachers, including science, history, folklore, and much else about the sun. sten odenwald’s “solar storms” site: https://www.solarstorms.org/ (https://www.solarstorms.org/). rich website with historical and modern information by the astronomer who wrote the book solar storms. stereo mission: https://stereo.gsfc.nasa.gov/ (https://stereo.gsfc.nasa.gov/). two nasa spacecraft monitoring the sun from different vantage points to provide more 3-d coverage of its activity.
