link to learning 212 6 • astronomical instruments access for free at openstax.org [image: astronomy_openstax_page230_img1.jpeg] gamma-ray detections can also be made from earth’s surface by using the atmosphere as the primary detector. when a gamma ray hits our atmosphere, it accelerates charged particles (mostly electrons) in the atmosphere. those energetic particles hit other particles in the atmosphere and give off their own radiation. the effect is a cascade of light and energy that can be detected on the ground. the veritas array in arizona and the h.e.s.s. array in namibia are two such ground-based gamma-ray observatories.
