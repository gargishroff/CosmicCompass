this artificial-color mosaic of 30 images taken with the chandra x-ray satellite shows a region 400 × 900 light-years in extent and centered on sagittarius a*, the bright white source in the center of the picture. the x-ray- emitting point sources are white dwarfs, neutron stars, and stellar black holes. the diffuse “haze” is emission from gas at a temperature of 10 million k. this hot gas is flowing away from the center out into the rest of the galaxy. the colors indicate x-ray energy bands: red (low energy), green (medium energy), and blue (high energy).
