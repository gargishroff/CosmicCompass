major meteor showers have 10 or more meteors per hour (zhr – zenith hourly rate). minor showers may have as few as two. radiants. meteors in a meteor shower appear to come from one point in the sky. this is called the radiant. this is an illusion. it is caused by the same effect that makes a pair of railroad tracks seem to meet in a single point on the distant horizon. the showers are named after their radiants. astronomy for mere mortals – an introductory astronomy text 228 three classes of meteorites: • stoney • stoney-iron • iron figure 266: an iron meteorite photo by h. raab figure 268: inside an iron meteorite photo by h. raab figure 265: inside a stoney meteorite photo by h. raab figure 267: inside a stoney-iron meteorite photo by c.v.schreibers note: meteor showers with thousands of meteors per hour are called meteor storms. note: the best time to view most meteor showers is when there is no moon in the sky, away from lights, and in the pre-dawn hours.
