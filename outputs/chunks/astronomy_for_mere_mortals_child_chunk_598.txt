indeed, the tychonic model is just as accurate as the copernican model, since they are mathematically equivalent (try erasing the orbit for the sun around the earth and then drawing an orbit for the earth around the sun: you have just converted tycho’s system into copernicus’ system). but kepler disagreed with tycho. kepler was totally convinced that heliocentrism was the true model of the heavens based on mystical pythagorean principles of symmetry and beauty. besides, tycho’s model would have the orbit of the sun cross the orbit of mars: how was that supposed to work? kepler was glad to be working with the best data in existence, but he hated that it was being used to support the tychonic model. this all ended when tycho, after a night of drinking and feasting, died of a burst bladder in october, 1601. after wrangling with tycho’s widow, kepler finally inherited tycho’s data. kepler’s war on mars johannes kepler (1571-1630) was the son of a soldier- of-fortune whom he barely knew, and a mother who owned a small property that would be the source of her sorrow.
