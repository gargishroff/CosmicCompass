laplace and the nebular hypothesis the late 18th – early 19th centuries witnessed more than just new telescopes and new observations. astronomical theory also evolved after newton’s theory of gravity. questions arose as to the origins of the solar system and how it developed. one line of speculation that had implications for current theories of solar system evolution was the nebular hypothesis. the nebular hypothesis was first suggested by scientist/theologian emanuel swedenborg in 1734, and expanded by philosopher immanuel kant in 1755. but it took on its most detailed and plausible form in the work of pierre-simon laplace. this version of the theory survived well into the 19th century.
