the details of galaxy collisions are complex, and the process can take hundreds of millions of years. thus, collisions are best simulated on a computer (figure 28.8), where astronomers can calculate the slow interactions of stars, and clouds of gas and dust, via gravity. these calculations show that if the collision is slow, the colliding galaxies may coalesce to form a single galaxy. figure 28.8 computer simulation of a galaxy collision. this computer simulation starts with two spiral galaxies merging and ends with a single elliptical galaxy. the colors show the colors of stars in the system; note the bursts of blue color as copious star formation gets triggered by the interaction. the timescale from start to finish in this sequence is about a billion years.
