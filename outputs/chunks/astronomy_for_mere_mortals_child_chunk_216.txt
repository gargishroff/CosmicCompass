venus’ greatest elongation as venus orbits the sun, as an inner planet, it never gets more than 47 ° away from the sun. this is its greatest elongation. venus’ synodic period the time it takes an object to progress from its full phase back to its full phase is its synodic period. venus’ synodic period is 583.92 days. because the phases or nodes are defined by the position of venus and the sun as well as the location of the earth, this takes longer than one orbit around the sun. as venus moves in its orbit, the earth is moving in its orbit as well. in order for venus to return to the same orientation relative to the earth and the sun it must travel further than just a single orbit to get there. when venus has completed its first orbit, the earth has moved around its orbit only 225 °. at the end of venus’ second orbit, the earth is now almost a quarter of a way into its second orbit. venus has not caught up with it yet. they align again after another 140 days, or a total of 584 days.
