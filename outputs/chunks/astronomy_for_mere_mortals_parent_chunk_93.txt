sirius is the brightest star in the nighttime sky. it has an apparent magnitude of -1.5. all other stars are fainter and therefore have larger (positive) apparent magnitudes. absolute magnitude an object’s absolute magnitude is a standard way to compare the brightness of objects. this is the apparent magnitude you would see if you were at a distance of 10 parsecs from the objects. astronomy for mere mortals – an introductory astronomy text 285 equation 19: luminosity vs. absolute magnitude 𝐿= 10(−𝑀−4.83 2.5 ) l = luminosity (solar units) m = absolute magnitude table 38: star numbers vs.
