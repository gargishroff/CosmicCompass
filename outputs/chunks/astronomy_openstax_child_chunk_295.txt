(a) if the moon did not rotate as it orbited earth, it would present all of its sides to our view; hence the white arrow would point directly toward earth only in the bottom position on the diagram. (b) actually, the moon rotates in the same period that it revolves, so we always see the same side (the white arrow keeps pointing to earth). the differences in the moon’s appearance from one night to the next are due to changing illumination by the sun, not to its own rotation.
