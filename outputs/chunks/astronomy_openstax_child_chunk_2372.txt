(a) the disk of whirling gas at right was discovered at the center of the giant elliptical galaxy m87 with the hubble space telescope. observations made on opposite sides of the disk show that one side is approaching us (the spectral lines are blueshifted by the doppler effect) while the other is receding (lines redshifted), a clear indication that the disk is rotating. the rotation speed is about 550 kilometers per second or 1.2 million miles per hour. such a high rotation speed is evidence that there is a very massive black hole at the center of m87.
