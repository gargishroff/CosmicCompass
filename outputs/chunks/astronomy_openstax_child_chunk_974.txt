while this does not change our general picture of europa’s internal structure, a thicker ice crust suggests that the deep ocean may be more difficult to access than we previously thought. if europa really has a large ocean of liquid water under its ice, then it may be the only place in the solar system, other than earth, with really large amounts of liquid water.1 to remain liquid, this ocean must be warmed by heat escaping from the interior of europa. hot (or at least warm) springs might be active there, analogous to those we have discovered in the deep oceans of earth. the necessary internal heat is generated by tidal heating (see the discussion later in this chapter). a short film (https://openstax.org/l/30europa) with planetary scientist kevin hand explains why europa is so interesting for future exploration. or listen to this more in-depth talk (https://openstax.org/l/30europa2) on europa. what makes the idea of an ocean with warm springs exciting is the discovery in earth’s oceans of large ecosystems clustered around deep ocean hot springs. such life derives all its energy from the mineral-laden water and thrives independent of the sunlight shining on earth’s surface.
