a famous example: why are there just six, and only six, planets? answer: because there are exactly five, and only five, platonic solids. the diagram shows the five platonic solids: the tetrahedron, octahedron, icosahedron, the cube, and the dodecahedron. the pythagoreans had proved that there could be no sixth perfect solid, that is, a solid where every facet is either an equilateral triangle, square, or pentagon. for kepler, this kind of perfection must be mirrored in the heavens, so he produced a model based on this notion. this diagram is from the mysterium cosmographicum showing how spaces between the six planets are filled with the five platonic solids. each sphere (here shown as half-spheres so you can see inside) carries a planet, and between each sphere is a platonic solid nested between them. this is typical mystical pythagorean reasoning, and kepler’s 1596 book is loaded with it.
