9.7.1 the discovery of the cosmic microwave background radio astronomers arno penzias and robert wilson serendipitously discovered this background radiation in 1963. however, incontrovertible proof as to its origin had to wait until 1992 when the cobe satellite was able to show that the background radiation had the precise black body spectrum that would have been expected (figure 9.6). it is worth telling a little of the nobel prize winning story of its discovery. penzias and wilson had been given use of the telescope and receiver that had been used for the very first passive satellite communication experiments using a large aluminium covered balloon called ‘echo’. it had been designed to minimize any extraneous noise that might enter the horn shaped telescope and the receiver was one of the best in the world at that time. they tested the system thoroughly and found that there was more background noise produced than they expected. they wondered if it might have been caused by pigeons nesting within the horn – the pigeons, being at ∼290 k, would radiate radio noise – and bought a pigeon trap (now in the smithsonian air and space museum in washington) to catch the pigeons.
