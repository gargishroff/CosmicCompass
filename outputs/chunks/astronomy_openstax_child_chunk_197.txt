objects like apples at the surface of earth, at a distance of one earth-radius from the center of earth, are observed to accelerate downward at 9.8 meters per second per second . it is this force of gravity on the surface of earth that gives us our sense of weight. unlike your mass, which would remain the same on any planet or moon, your weight depends on the local force of gravity. so you would weigh less on mars and the moon than on earth, even though there is no change in your mass. (which means you would still have to go easy on the desserts in the college cafeteria when you got back!) the moon is 60 earth radii away from the center of earth. if gravity (and the acceleration it causes) gets weaker with distance squared, the acceleration the moon experiences should be a lot less than for the apple. the acceleration should be (or 3600 times less—about ). this is precisely the observed acceleration of the moon in its orbit.
