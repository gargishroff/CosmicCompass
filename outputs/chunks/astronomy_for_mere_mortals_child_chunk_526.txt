comparative planetology the study of the planets by comparing them to one another, especially comparing them to earth. condensation theory the scientific theory that describes how planets could form from bits of matter in the solar nebula that stuck together or condensed. it is an add-on to the basic nebular theory. conjunction the point when a superior planet is on the opposite side of the sun from the earth. contact binary a binary star system where both stars have expanded beyond their roche lobes and are sharing material with each other. continental drift the motion of large chunks of the earth’s crust. also known as plate tectonics. continuous spectrum the light emitted from a hot solid, liquid, or dense gas. convection the boiling motion of the earth’s atmosphere as warm air rises and cooler air drops. convection zone the region of the sun’s interior outside the radiation zone. the material in this region is churning like a pot of boiling water. core • the center of the earth or any other differentiated object. often it has an inner core and an outer core. • the center of the sun or any other star.. the place where the fusion takes place. corona the outer solar atmosphere. it is only visible during a total solar eclipse.
