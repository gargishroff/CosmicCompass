(a) this cassini image shows the narrow, complex f ring of saturn, with its two small shepherd moons pandora (left) and prometheus (right). (b) in this closer view, the shepherd moon pandora (84 kilometers across) is seen next to the f ring, in which the moon is perturbing the main (brightest) strand of ring particles as it passes. you can see the dark side of pandora on this image because it is being illuminated by the light reflected from saturn. (credit a, b: modification of work by nasa/jpl/space science institute) you can download a movie (https://openstax.org/l/30shprdmns) showing the two shepherd moons on either side of saturn’s f ring. theoretical calculations suggest that the other narrow rings in the uranian and neptunian systems should also be controlled by shepherd moons, but none has been located. the calculated diameter for such shepherds (about 10 kilometers) was just at the limit of detectability for the voyager cameras, so it is impossible to say whether they are present or not.
