when accurate measurements of stellar brightness were made in the nineteenth century it became apparent that, on average, the stars of a given magnitude were approximately 2.5 times brighter than those of the next fainter magnitude and that 1st magnitude stars were about 100 times brighter than the 6th magnitude stars.
