the four giant planets in our solar system all have hydrogen atmospheres, but the warm gas giants, jupiter and saturn, have tan, beige, red, and white clouds that are thought to be composed of ammonia ice particles with various colorants called “chromophores.” the blue-tinted ice giants, uranus and neptune, are much colder and covered in methane ice clouds.
