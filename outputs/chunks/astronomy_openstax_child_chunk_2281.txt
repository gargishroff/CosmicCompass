(credit a: modification of work by hubble legacy archive, nasa, esa, and judy schmidt; credit b: modification of work by “jschulman555”/ wikimedia) in galaxies that we see face on, the bright stars and emission nebulae make the arms of spirals stand out like those of a pinwheel on the fourth of july. open star clusters can be seen in the arms of nearer spirals, and globular clusters are often visible in their halos. spiral galaxies contain a mixture of young and old stars, just as the milky way does. all spirals rotate, and the direction of their spin is such that the arms appear to trail much like the wake of a boat.
