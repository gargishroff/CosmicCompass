(that way, they would not have to produce so much energy to look as bright as they do.) one way was to find a “mismatched pair”—a quasar and a galaxy with different redshifts that lie in very nearly the same direction in the sky. suppose you do find one and only one galaxy with a quasar very close by, and the redshift of the quasar is six times larger than that of the galaxy. have your group discuss whether you could then conclude that the two objects are at the same distance and that redshift is not a reliable indicator of distance. why? suppose you found three such pairs, each with different mismatched redshifts?
