[image: astronomy_for_mere_mortals_page345_img1.png] astronomy for mere mortals – an introductory astronomy text 346 figure 424: an open, closed, or flat universe the expansion of the universe is accelerating we live in a slightly open universe geometry of the universe but, as we see from einstein’s theories, space can be warped by high mass objects. so too, space can be warped by the mass of the universe. a universe that is at the critical density (ω0) is a flat universe or a critical universe. objects in a flat universe will follow newton’s laws and behave as we would expect based on euclidean geometry. as we were taught, a triangle drawn in a flat universe will have angles adding up to 180 °. in two dimensions this is like a flat sheet of paper. but if the universe is a high density universe, then space will be curved and this is called a closed universe. the two dimensional equivalent would be the surface of a sphere such as the earth. a triangle drawn on the surface of a sphere will have angles that total more than 180 ° (see figure 424). to visualize this, start at the north pole. travel south to the equator and turn eastward.
