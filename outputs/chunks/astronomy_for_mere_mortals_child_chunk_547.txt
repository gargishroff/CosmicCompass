prominences loops and sheets of the sun’s materials that rise above the surface of the sun along the magnetic field lines. they may last for days. proton-proton chain the nuclear fusion reaction that combines protons to produce helium. it is the more common process believed to occur in smaller stars. protons not so small positively charged particles that exist in the nucleus of an atom. protoplanets larger objects that are almost formed into planets. astronomy for mere mortals – an introductory astronomy text 409 protostar the stage of a star’s life after it has collapsed into a sphere, but before it has begun nuclear fusion. protostellar disk the disk of material surrounding a protostar that will eventually form into the planets. protostellar winds the strong winds that radiate from stars while they are forming and in the protostar stage. these often appear as jets of material streaming from the poles of the protostar. pulsar a rapidly rotating neutron star when the magnetic axis is different from the rotation axis. material that is ejected from the magnetic poles rotates around and once each cycle is pointed at the earth. quark a tiny particle of which protons and neutrons are composed.
