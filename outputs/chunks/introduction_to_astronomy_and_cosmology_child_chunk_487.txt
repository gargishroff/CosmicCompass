[image: introduction_to_astronomy_and_cosmology_page269_img1.jpeg] stellar evolution – the life and death of stars 249 the proton decays into a positron and neutral pion which then immediately decays into two gamma rays. the positron will annihilate with an electron to form two more gamma rays. to this end, a number of detectors were built in the 1980s including that at the kamioka underground observatory located 1000 m below ground in japan. to provide the protons, 3000 tons of pure water was contained in a cylinder 16 m tall and 15.6 m in diameter. the cylinder was surrounded by 1000 photomulti- plier tubes attached to its inner surface which would be able to detect the gamma rays produced in the proton decay. it came into operation in 1983 and was given greater sensitivity in 1985.
