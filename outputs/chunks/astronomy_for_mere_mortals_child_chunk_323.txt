astronomy for mere mortals – an introductory astronomy text 255 figure 300: a basic hr diagram [image: astronomy_for_mere_mortals_page255_img1.png] astronomy for mere mortals – an introductory astronomy text 256 figure 301: hr diagram with spectral types the sun is a type g2v star [image: astronomy_for_mere_mortals_page256_img1.jpeg] astronomy for mere mortals – an introductory astronomy text 257 spectral regions figure 302 is an hr diagram that shows the regions in which different types of stars can be found. the blue oval at the top is the realm of the supergiants. the pink oval near the right side is the realm of the giants. the green oval near the bottom is the realm of the white dwarfs. note that there is a gray band running from the center of the bottom to the top right. this is called the instability strip. stars that are found within this region are not stable stars. they tend to oscillate and include the variable stars. the small purple oval in this region is the realm of the rr lyrae variables stars, and the larger dark yellow oval is the realm of the cepheid variable stars.
