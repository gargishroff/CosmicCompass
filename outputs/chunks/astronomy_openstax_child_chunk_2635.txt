a different kind of search for wimps is being conducted at the large hadron collider (lhc) at cern, europe’s particle physics lab near geneva, switzerland. in this experiment, protons collide with enough energy potentially to produce wimps. the lhc detectors cannot detect the wimps directly, but if wimps are produced, they will pass through the detectors, carrying energy away with them. experimenters will then add up all the energy that they detect as a result of the collisions of protons to determine if any energy is missing. so far, none of these experiments has detected wimps. will the newer experiments pay off? or will scientists have to search for some other explanation for dark matter? only time will tell (figure 29.23). figure 29.23 dark matter. this cartoon from nasa takes a humorous look at how little we yet understand about dark matter.
