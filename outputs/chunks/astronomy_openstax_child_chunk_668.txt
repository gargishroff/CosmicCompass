link to learning 8.4 • life, chemical evolution, and climate change 275 [image: astronomy_openstax_page293_img1.jpeg] human impacts on our planet earth is so large and has been here for so long that some people have trouble accepting that humans are really changing the planet, its atmosphere, and its climate. they are surprised to learn, for example, that the carbon dioxide released from burning fossil fuels is 100 times greater than that emitted by volcanoes. but, the data clearly tell the story that our climate is changing rapidly, and that almost all of the change is a result of human activity. this is not the first time that humans have altered our environment dramatically. some of the greatest changes were caused by our ancestors, before the development of modern industrial society. if aliens had visited earth 50,000 years ago, they would have seen much of the planet supporting large animals of the sort that now survive only in africa. the plains of australia were occupied by giant marsupials such as diprotodon and zygomaturus (the size of our elephants today), and a species of kangaroo that stood 10 feet high.
