714 21 • the birth of stars and the discovery of planets outside the solar system access for free at openstax.org figure 21.15 dust ring around a young star and three nested dust belts around the star fomalhaut. (a) this image was made by alma (the atacama large millimeter/submillimeter array) at a wavelength of 1.3 millimeters and shows the young star hl tau and its protoplanetary disk. it reveals multiple rings and gaps that indicate the presence of emerging planets, which are sweeping their orbits clear of dust and gas. (b) the james webb space telescope took this infrared image of three belts in the debris disk of the hot, young star fomalhaut, about 25 lightyears away. the belts extend outward for about 150 au or 23 billion km, roughly twice the size of our own kuiper belt. although the disk was discovered in 1983, the presence of the inner belts was revealed by jwst for the first time.
