planet a body that is orbiting a star (or the sun) that must meet these three requirements in our solar system: massive enough to become basically a sphere, orbit the sun, and are massive enough to have cleared their orbit of debris. planetary nebula the final result of oscillations in a sun-like star resulting in the outer layers of the atmosphere being blown off in a growing bubble. the core collapses into a white dwarf. planetary transits a common technique for detecting planets around other stars as they move across the face of their star and cause a slight dimming in the star’s luminosity. planetesimals small objects that continue to grow into planets. planck epoch the first 10-43 seconds after the big bang. our theories can’t tell us what occurred at this time. all four fundamental forces were unified at this time. plate tectonics the motion of large chunks of the earth’s crust. also known as continental drift. polarized light light waves oriented in the same direction. prime focus the point where the image is in focus in a reflecting telescope after only bouncing off the primary mirror. prism a triangular piece of glass in a spectroscope that causes the different frequencies of light to separate.
