(b) a prominence is a huge cloud of relatively cool (about 60,000 k in this case), fairly dense gas suspended in the much hotter corona. these pictures, taken in ultraviolet, are color coded so that white corresponds to the hottest temperatures and dark red to cooler ones. the four images were taken, moving clockwise from the upper left, on may 15, 2001; march 28, 2000; january 18, 2000; and february 2, 2001. (credit a: modification of work by nasa/soho; credit b: modification of work by nasa/sdo) flares and coronal mass ejections the most violent event on the surface of the sun is a rapid eruption called a solar flare (figure 15.20). a typical flare lasts for 5 to 10 minutes and releases a total amount of energy equivalent to that of perhaps a million hydrogen bombs. the largest flares last for several hours and emit enough energy to power the entire united states at its current rate of electrical consumption for 100,000 years.
