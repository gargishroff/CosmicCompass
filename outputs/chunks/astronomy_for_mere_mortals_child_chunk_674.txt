clyde william tombaugh (1906-1997) was the son of a farmer in streator, illinois, who got bitten by the astronomy bug as a boy. his plans to attend college were thwarted, however, when a hail storm destroyed an entire crop, plunging his family into debt. beginning in 1926 tombaugh began building a series of telescopes from materials he could scrounge, and he made sketches of his observations of mars and jupiter. he sent some of his work to the lowell observatory in flagstaff, arizona, which was famous for its work on [image: astronomy_for_mere_mortals_page469_img1.jpeg] astronomy for mere mortals – an introductory astronomy text 470 figure 509: lowell observatory telescope photo by lowell observatory figure 510: percival lowell public domain mars. he was hoping for advice on how to improve his methods. what he got in return was a job offer. when tombaugh arrived in flagstaff in january, 1929, he was taken under the wing of the observatory’s director, vesto slipher.
