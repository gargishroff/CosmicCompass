(usually, we use metric units and not the old british system that the united states is still using, but we are going to humor your uncle and stick with miles.) if light travels 186,000 miles every second, then it will travel 60 times that in a minute, and 60 times that in an hour, and 24 times that in a day, and 365 times that in a year. so we have 1.86 × 105 × 6.0 × 101 × 6.0 × 101 × 2.4 × 101 × 3.65 × 102. so we multiply all the numbers out front together and add all the exponents. we get 586.57 × 1010 = 5.86 × 1012 miles in a light year (which is roughly 6 trillion miles—a heck of a lot of miles). so if the star is 60 light-years away, its distance in miles is 6 × 101 × 5.86 × 1012 = 35.16 × 1013 = 3.516 × 1014 miles.
