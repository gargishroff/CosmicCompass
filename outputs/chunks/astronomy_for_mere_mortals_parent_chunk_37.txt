astronomy for mere mortals – an introductory astronomy text 111 component 34 – redshifts while it makes sense to talk about distances on earth in terms of kilometers and miles, distances in the solar system in aus, and distances between the stars in light-years and parsecs, when astronomers talk about very distant objects they often use the measurement of redshift. red-shift is a function of the recessional velocity, which can be calculated based on the doppler shift in the wavelength of the light emitted by the object. as we look at objects that are farther away, we have to take into account the time it took for the light to reach us. if it is receding from us, then during the time it took for the light to reach us, it has moved even farther away. to avoid any confusion when we talk about distances to these objects, it is simpler to refer to the apparent distance as the lookback time.
