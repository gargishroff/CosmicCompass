28.4 the challenge of dark matter learning objectives by the end of this section, you will be able to: so far this chapter has focused almost entirely on matter that radiates electromagnetic energy—stars, planets, gas, and dust. but, as we have pointed out in several earlier chapters (especially the milky way galaxy), it is now clear that galaxies contain large amounts of dark matter as well. there is much more dark matter, in fact, than matter we can see—which means it would be foolish to ignore the effect of this unseen material in our theories about the structure of the universe. (as many a ship captain in the polar seas found out too late, the part of the iceberg visible above the ocean’s surface was not necessarily the only part he needed to pay attention to.) dark matter turns out to be extremely important in determining the evolution of galaxies and of the universe as a whole.
