for example, craters on venus are named for women who have made significant contributions to human knowledge and welfare. volcanic features on jupiter’s moon io, which is in a constant state of volcanic activity, are named after gods of fire and thunder from the mythologies of many cultures. craters on mercury commemorate famous novelists, playwrights, artists, and composers. on saturn’s moon tethys, all the features are named after characters and places in homer’s great epic poem, the odyssey. as we explore further, it may well turn out that more places in the solar system need names than earth history can provide. perhaps by then, explorers and settlers on these worlds will be ready to develop their own names for the places they may (if but for a while) call home. you may be surprised to know that the meaning of the word planet has recently become controversial because we have discovered many other planetary systems that don’t look very much like our own. even within our solar system, the planets differ greatly in size and chemical properties.
