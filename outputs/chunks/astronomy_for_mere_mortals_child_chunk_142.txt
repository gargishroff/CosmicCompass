all stars can be placed on the hertzsprung-russell diagram (hr diagram) (see figure ) based on their electromagnetic peak frequency and their intensity. once placed on the hr diagram, their location will tell us the absolute magnitude. when we observe the star, we can measure the apparent magnitude. knowing these two values we can calculate the distance. (there will be more on hr diagrams later.) we use a formula called the distance modulus. this equation lets us input the values for the apparent brightness and the absolute brightness and results in the distance in parsecs.
