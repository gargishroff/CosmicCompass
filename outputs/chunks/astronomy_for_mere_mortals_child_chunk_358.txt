[image: astronomy_for_mere_mortals_page282_img1.png] [image: astronomy_for_mere_mortals_page282_img2.png] astronomy for mere mortals – an introductory astronomy text 283 s-process = 1 neutron per year r-process = 1 neutron per second s-process makes atoms to bismuth r-process makes atoms to uranium equation 18: neutron creation 13c + 4he → 16o + n 22ne + 4he → 25mg + n component 119 – fusion in the r-process at the end of a large star’s life, as the supernova is occuring, there is another fusion process that occurs. it is called the r-process, or the rapid process of neutron capture (rapid process). the process is very similar to the s-process. neutrons are captured by the nucleus until it becomes unstable and decays. the difference is that the r-process requires less time for each successive neutron capture. rather than a rate of one neutron capture per year (as in the s- process), the r-process proceeds at an average rate of one neutron capture per second. this means that more isotopes are stable enough to permit the process to continue to higher-mass nuclei before decay occurs.
