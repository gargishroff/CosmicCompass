herschel measures the galaxy in 1785, william herschel (figure 25.2) made the first important discovery about the architecture of the milky way galaxy. using a large reflecting telescope that he had built, william and his sister caroline counted stars in different directions of the sky. they found that most of the stars they could see lay in a flattened structure encircling the sky, and that the numbers of stars were about the same in any direction around this structure. herschel therefore concluded that the stellar system to which the sun belongs has the shape of a disk or wheel (he might have called it a frisbee except frisbees hadn’t been invented yet), and that the sun must be near the hub of the wheel (figure 25.3). figure 25.2 william herschel (1738–1822) and caroline herschel (1750–1848). william herschel was a german musician who emigrated to england and took up astronomy in his spare time.
