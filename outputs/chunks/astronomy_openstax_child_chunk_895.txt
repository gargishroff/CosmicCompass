the probe plunged at a shallow angle into jupiter’s atmosphere, traveling at a speed of 50 kilometers per second—that’s fast enough to fly from new york to san francisco in 100 seconds! this was the highest speed at which any probe has so far entered the atmosphere of a planet, and it put great demands on the heat shield protecting it. the high entry speed was a result of acceleration by the strong gravitational attraction of jupiter. link to learning making connections 11.1 • exploring the outer planets 369 atmospheric friction slowed the probe within 2 minutes, producing temperatures at the front of its heat shield as high as 15,000 °c. as the probe’s speed dropped to 2500 kilometers per hour, the remains of the glowing heat shield were jettisoned, and a parachute was deployed to lower the instrumented probe spacecraft more gently into the atmosphere (figure 11.3). the data from the probe instruments were relayed to earth via the main galileo spacecraft. figure 11.3 galileo probe falling into jupiter and juno image of jupiter's south pole.
