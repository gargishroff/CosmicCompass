5.13.2 submillimetre wavelengths submillimetre wavelengths lie between infrared light and radio waves on the wavelength scale. this largely comes from cold material in the universe, such as the clouds of gas and dust found between the stars that form the ‘interstellar medium’. it is out of this dust and gas that new stars are born, and into which stars disperse material as they explode at the end of their lives. figure 5.29 the eskimo planetary nebula imaged in visible light by the hubble space telescope (left) and in the infrared by the united kingdom infrared telescope (right). image: hubble space telescope institute, nasa and chris davis and tim carroll (joint astronomy centre).
