radio telescope a telescope designed to detect electromagnetic radiation in the radio part of the spectrum. radio waves the wavelengths at the long end of the spectrum. rayleigh scattering the scattering of blue light in earth’s atmosphere. this is why the sky is blue. red giants large red stars in the final stages of their lives. red shift when an object is moving away from you, the lines in the astronomy for mere mortals – an introductory astronomy text 410 light being emitted are moved in the red direction (longer wavelengths). red shift surveys surveys of the universe studying the red shift of galaxies in a specific area to determine the distribution of galaxies. reddening dust and gas think enough to cause the scattering of blue light from distance stars before it can reach earth. this results in these stars appearing to be redder than they really are. reflection nebulae (singular is nebula) clouds of dust and gas that glow in the blue from photons that are scattered as they pass through. this is due to rayleigh scattering and is blue for the same reason that the sky on earth is blue. reflector a telescope that uses primarily mirrors to focus light. refractor a telescope that uses primarily lenses to focus light.
