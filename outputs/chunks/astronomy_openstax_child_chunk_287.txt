the pictures of the moon on the blue circle show the perspective from space, with the sun off to the right in a fixed position. the outer images show how the moon appears to you in the sky from each point in the orbit. imagine yourself standing on earth, facing the moon at each stage. in the position “new,” for example, you are facing the moon from the right side of earth in the middle of the day. (note that the distance of the moon from earth is not to scale in this diagram: the moon is roughly 30 earth-diameters away from us.) (credit: modification of work by nasa) the moon is said to be new when it is in the same general direction in the sky as the sun (position a). here, its illuminated (bright) side is turned away from us and its dark side is turned toward us. you might say that the sun is shining on the “wrong ” side of the moon from our perspective. in this phase the moon is invisible to us; its dark, rocky surface does not give off any light of its own. because the new moon is in the same part of the sky as the sun, it rises at sunrise and sets at sunset.
