the first black hole binary system to be discovered is called cygnus x-1 (see figure 24.1). the visible star in this binary system is spectral type o. measurements of the doppler shifts of the o star’s spectral lines show that it has an unseen companion. the x-rays flickering from it strongly indicate that the companion is a small collapsed object. the mass of the invisible collapsed companion is about 21 times that of the sun. the companion is therefore too massive to be either a white dwarf or a neutron star. a number of other binary systems also meet all the conditions for containing a black hole. table 24.1 lists the characteristics of some of the best examples. some black hole candidates in binary star systems name/catalog designation2 companion star spectral type orbital period (days) black hole mass estimates (msun) lmc x-1 o giant 3.9 10.9 cygnus x-1 o supergiant 5.6 21 table 24.1 2 as you can tell, there is no standard way of naming these candidates.
