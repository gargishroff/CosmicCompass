the pleiades open cluster in taurus is a great example of a reflection nebula (figure 332, figure 331). the light we see as blue is being scattered by the dust and gas in the neighborhood of the young stars in the open star cluster. reflection nebulae are more difficult to see in a telescope than are emission nebulae. sometimes both nebulae can be seen in the same dust cloud as in the trifid nebula in figure 331.
