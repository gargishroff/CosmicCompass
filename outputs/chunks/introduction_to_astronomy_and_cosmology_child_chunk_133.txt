(aurora is the roman god- dess of the dawn, boreas is the greek name for the north wind, and australis is the latin word for south.) the aurora borealis is often called the northern lights as it tends to be seen as a green or reddish glow in the northern sky. it is most commonly seen around the vernal and autumnal equinoxes, though it is not known why this should be so.
