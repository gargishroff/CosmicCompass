but in 2024, an international team of astronomers, led by claes fransson of stockholm university, announced that the james webb space telescope had now enabled them to look through the dust. using infrared light, they observed highly ionized atoms of argon and sulfur in the center of the supernova remnant. the argon atoms had lost five of their 18 electrons, a process that requires a photon with a lot of energy. (we discussed light as a photon in the behavior of light.) they tried to fit their observations with a number of different scenarios for where such high-energy photons could come from, but the only idea that explained all their observations is that there is a newly-born neutron star in the center giving off high-energy x-ray radiation. we will discuss the properties of such neutron stars in the section that follows. astronomers are delighted to be able to observe a newly-born neutron star so early in its life and will be following the story of this “star corpse” for many years to come.
